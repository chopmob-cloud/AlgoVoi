AlgoVoi Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 11, 2026

Short version: AlgoVoi stores your encrypted wallet data only on your own device. We do not collect, transmit, or sell any personal information.

1. Who We Are

AlgoVoi is an open-source Chrome extension developed by Chopmob Cloud. It provides a non-custodial Web3 wallet for the Algorand and Voi blockchain networks, along with native support for the x402 HTTP payment protocol. The source code is publicly available at github.com/chopmob-cloud/AlgoVoi.

2. Data We Do Not Collect

AlgoVoi does not:

  • Collect, transmit, or store any personally identifiable information on external servers.
  • Track your browsing activity, visited URLs, or transaction history on any server we control.
  • Sell, share, or license any user data to third parties.
  • Use analytics, advertising, or crash-reporting services.
  • Require account registration or email address.

3. Data Stored Locally on Your Device

All sensitive data is stored exclusively in your browser's chrome.storage.local, which is private to the extension and never accessible to websites you visit.

  • Encrypted wallet vault — Your private keys and seed phrases are encrypted using PBKDF2 (600,000 iterations) key derivation followed by AES-GCM 256-bit encryption before being written to local storage. The plaintext is never written to disk.
  • Wallet preferences — Active account index, selected network (Algorand / Voi), and UI settings are stored unencrypted in local storage.
  • Session state — WalletConnect pairing URIs and pending transaction queues are held in memory during your browser session and are not persisted after the extension service worker is unloaded.

4. Network Requests Made by the Extension

AlgoVoi communicates only with the following external services, solely to fulfill wallet functionality. No personal data accompanies these requests beyond what is inherent to blockchain transactions (e.g., your public wallet address).

  • Algorand node & indexer (mainnet-api.algonode.cloud, mainnet-idx.algonode.cloud) — to query balances, submit signed transactions, and retrieve on-chain data.
  • Voi node & indexer (mainnet-api.voi.nodely.dev, mainnet-idx.voi.nodely.dev) — same purpose for the Voi network.
  • WalletConnect relay (*.walletconnect.com, *.walletconnect.org) — to enable QR-code wallet pairing with dApps via the WalletConnect v2 protocol. Only encrypted session payloads are transmitted; WalletConnect does not receive your private keys.
  • x402 payment endpoints — when you approve an x402 payment, the extension constructs and submits a signed Algorand/Voi transaction to the merchant's endpoint. Only the signed transaction (which contains your public address) is transmitted; your private key never leaves your device.

5. Permissions Used and Why

  • storage — to persist the encrypted vault and preferences locally.
  • alarms — to schedule periodic session keep-alive checks in the service worker.
  • tabs — to open the WalletConnect pairing page in the extension popup and to communicate with the active page during x402 payment flows.
  • windows — to open the x402 payment approval popup window.
  • Host permissions (Algorand, Voi, WalletConnect domains) — to make API calls to the blockchain nodes and WalletConnect relay listed above.
  • Content script on https://*/* — to inject the window.algorand ARC-0027 provider and x402 fetch interceptor into web pages, allowing dApps to request transaction signing. The content script cannot read page content and only passes signing requests to the extension background.

6. Children's Privacy

AlgoVoi is not directed at children under 13 years of age. We do not knowingly collect any information from children.
