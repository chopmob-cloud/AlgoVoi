Compliance Summary

AlgoVoi-Hand operates as an x402 payment gateway. This page summarises its alignment with UK PSR 2017/PSD2, UK MLRs 2017, SAMLA 2018, PoCA 2002, and related frameworks. Full audit documentation is available on request under NDA.

Sanctions Screening (C1)

Every payment is screened against a locally cached, indexed database of sanctions entries before the transaction is persisted. Feeds are refreshed daily from three public sources: OFSI (HM Treasury — UK SAMLA 2018 designations), OFAC SDN (US Treasury), and EU Consolidated sanctions list. Wallet addresses are normalised and matched in a single indexed lookup. Hits result in an automatic ScreeningHit record with action taken (blocked or escalated) and MLRO notification. Generic error responses prevent tipping-off (SAMLA 2018 s.20).

Tenant wallet addresses are also screened on KYB approval and on risk tier changes. Risk tier enforcement gates all payments: restricted tenants receive a generic 403 with no disclosure; high-risk tenants have daily volume caps reduced by 50%.

Transaction Monitoring (C2)

A configurable rules engine evaluates each payment post-confirmation against six seeded rules: large single transaction (≥ $10k), high 24h volume (≥ $50k), high frequency per hour (≥ 100 tx), high payer frequency (structuring signal — ≥ 20 tx/hour), plus two additional configurable thresholds. Rule matches produce ComplianceEvent records with action alert, block, or escalate. Escalations automatically notify the MLRO and set aml_hold=TRUE on the linked payment. Monitoring is post-commit and wrapped in a try/except — it never crashes the payment path.

AML Data Retention (C3)

Payment records linked to a ScreeningHit or an escalation ComplianceEvent are flagged aml_hold=TRUE via a Postgres trigger and application-layer enforcement. These records are excluded from the 90-day GDPR purge reaper. aml_retention_until is set to verified_at + 5 years, satisfying UK MLRs Reg 40(1)(b). High-risk tenant payments are also held by default per AML_AUTO_HOLD_HIGH_RISK_TENANTS.

KYB Document Workflow (C4)

Merchants onboard via a structured KYB document checklist tailored to their legal entity type. Required documents for UK limited companies include: Certificate of Incorporation, Proof of Address, PSC / UBO Register, Director Passport, and Source of Funds declaration. Documents are uploaded directly to Cloudflare R2 (zero-egress, UK/EU data residency) via presigned PUT URLs with 15-minute TTL — file bytes never transit the application server.

An approval gate (KYB_ENFORCE_DOCUMENT_GATE) prevents kyb_status=approved unless all required documents have been accepted. All KYB documents are retained under aml_hold=TRUE until the business relationship ends + 5 years (MLRs Reg 40(2)).

MLRO & SAR Workflow (C5)

A designated Money Laundering Reporting Officer (MLRO) is assigned at the platform level. Escalation events and sanctions hits trigger immediate MLRO notification by email and webhook. Internal SAR draft/submit/acknowledge records are maintained with full audit trail and restricted=TRUE flag — SAR existence is never exposed to tenants or payers, satisfying PoCA 2002 s.330 non-disclosure requirements. Actual submission is via UKFIU SARs Online (no public API available).
